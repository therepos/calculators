Definitions ▾
NSR (Net Standard Revenue) = Σ (Hours × NSR Rate/hr). The standard value of resources deployed. NSR rates are fixed for the financial year per rank/country/service line.

ANSR (Adjusted Net Standard Revenue) = the collectible fees agreed with the client. ANSR = NSR × (1 + EAF%). This is the revenue recognized from time charges.

EAF (Engagement Adjustment Factor) = (ANSR ÷ NSR) − 1. The premium or discount vs standard rates. Positive = charging above standard. Negative = discount. One EAF applies to all hours on the engagement.

TER (Total Engagement Revenue) = ANSR + Billable Expenses. The total amount collectible from the client.

Margin = ANSR − (Labour Cost + Tech Fee + Expenses). Labour cost uses actual cost rates per rank, not NSR. Tech Fee = 0.5% of labour cost (varies by service code).

NUI (Net Unbilled Inventory) = (Recognized ANSR + Charged Expenses) − (Billed Fees + Billed Expenses).
  NUI > 0 → work done but not yet billed (collection risk)
  NUI = 0 → billed exactly what has been recognized
  NUI < 0 → billed ahead of work performed (advance/milestone billing)

Budget Cost Allowance = Fee × (1 − Target Margin%). The maximum you can spend to hit your margin target. If Total Projected Cost exceeds this, margin will be below target.
